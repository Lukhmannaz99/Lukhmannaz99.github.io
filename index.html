04 · Local Relevance

Malaysian Regulatory Awareness

My courses do not cover Malaysian regulations directly — so I took the initiative to self-study the local regulatory landscape. Here is what I understand about the key regulations employers in Malaysia will expect me to know.

PDPA

Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (Act 709)

Data privacy law for commercial entities

Governs collection, processing, and storage of personal data. I have studied the 7 principles and upcoming PDPD amendments including mandatory breach notification and DPO requirements.

RMiT

BNM Risk Management in Technology Policy

Cybersecurity regulation for financial institutions

Bank Negara Malaysia's primary tech risk policy. I understand its structure covering Governance, Technology Risk, Cyber Risk, Cloud/Outsourcing, and Resilience — relevant for banking/finance GRC roles.

SC

Securities Commission Cyber Risk Guidelines

Cyber risk rules for capital market participants

Guidelines on Management of Cyber Risk applicable to brokers, fund managers, and market intermediaries. Aligned to the Identify-Protect-Detect-Respond-Recover structure of NIST CSF.

NACSA

National Cyber Security Agency Malaysia

National cybersecurity coordinator & CII regulator

Oversees Critical Information Infrastructure (CII) sectors. I am aware of mandatory incident reporting to NACSA/MyCERT and the National Cyber Security Policy (NCSP) 2020.

CCA

Computer Crimes Act 1997 (Act 563)

Criminalises unauthorised computer access

Malaysia's primary cybercrime law. Relevant to GRC for defining legal liability around unauthorised access, data modification, and wrongful communication of access codes.

MCMC

Malaysian Communications & Multimedia Commission

Telecoms & internet sector regulator

Regulates communications networks under CMA 1998. Security provisions relevant to telcos and ISPs; coordinates with NACSA on national cybersecurity incidents.
